HP Spectre x360

By Wayne Harrison

Wrong review? Go back.

Introduction

In this review i'll be looking at the HP Spectre x360, a powerhouse of a laptop with great aesthetics and an impressive display. So does it perform as well as it looks? Lets find out.

The Good

There are a lot of good things that this laptop has to offer. To start with the specs of the product are impressive for a laptop, packing a seventh generation Intel Core i7 and an nVidia GTX 940MX it will handle all office tasks with ease and even handle light gaming if you ever want to relax after work or studying. The Spectre x360 also comes with 8GB of RAM allowing for more than enough memory for office tasks and even light editing tasks. Storage on the Spectre is no slouch either with, depending on the model, 256GB-1TB of PCIe SSD storage rated at transfer speeds of over 1GB/s. The input devices on the Spectre are great too, the keyboard is easy to use with a short travel and it overall performs well and feels somewhat familiar, the trackpad is large and makes for a good browsing experience using it. The mouse doesn't get knocked when you accidentally touch it with the palm of your hand and even has a satisfying tactile click when selecting things. Another impressive feature is the IPS 4K display, text is crisp, viewing angles are immense. It is hard to move back to a lower resolution screen after working on this laptop for a while.

TL;DR

  • Technical specs are amazing for a small form factor laptop.
  • Keyboard and trackpad are very well designed and feel excellent to use.
  • The display is crisp and has amazing viewing angles.

The Bad

The first major put off for most people is going to be the price of this laptop it is a premium product and as such it is priced as one. Selling for £1,299.00 for the base model and £1.899.00 for the top of the line model, this laptop does not come cheap at all. Another issue is that whilst the touch screen and pen can be useful it is very niche and probably won't see much use in every day use, but if you do need it it is there and available to be used. The laptop can also be folded in half and be used in tablet mode which honestly feels like a useless feature, some might find this to be useful but at the time of writing this review I honestly can say that, outside of testing it, I have never used the tablet mode. Something else that is a more of an annoyance is the hinges on the Spectre they feel solid, but the slighest knock or jolt or even pressing hard on the keyboard can make the screen have a slight wobble.

TL;DR

  • The Spectre is very expensive.
  • Touchscreen and pen functionality feels redundant.
  • The tablet mode feels like a useless feature.

Conclusion

So do the positives outweight the negatives? In my opinion yes they do. The tech specs are amazing for a small laptop. It has plenty of storage and enough memory to get the job done for light editing and all office tasks and is a powerhouse of a laptop and is well worth the asking price.
