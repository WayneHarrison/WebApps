Viewsonic VX2475Smhl-4K

By Wayne Harrison

Introduction

The Viewsonic VX2475Smhl-4K a 4K monitor with a presence, crisp colours and a good refresh rate, but is it worth the price tag?

The Good

The colours that this monitor offers are great, supporting true 8-bit colour without dithering, blacks are dark and lighter colours vibrant making for a great viewing experience. The inputs on the Viewsonic are plentiful, supporting HDMI 2.0 and DP 1.2 allowing for a 4k 60Hz experience, whilst earlier revisions of the ports only allow for a 30Hz experience. The price is good for what you are getting aswell, retailing at around Â£300 this monitor shows that you don't have to pay top price for a high quality viewing experience.

TL;DR

  • Good colours, supports true 8-bit colour.
  • Good selection of inputs including HDMI 2.0 and DP 1.2.
  • Priced low versus other 4K monitors that are available.

The Bad

The bezel on the Viewsonic are quite thick when compared to some other monitors making this a poor multiscreen monitor if you want more of a seemless multi-monitor setup. Whilst the price is low compared to other 4k monitors it still is rather expensive when compared to some other, lower resolution, monitors that are on the market.

TL;DR

  • Large bezels might put some people off.
  • Still expensive when compared to lower resolution monitors.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Viewsonic VX2475Smhl-4k is a monitor with great colours, future proof inputs and it, in my opinion, is well worth the asking price.
