Logitech G502

By Wayne Harrison

Wrong review? Go back.

Introduction

The Logitech G502 a middle of the pack mouse, but is that all there is to it? Find out more in the review below.

The Good

The G502 is an exceptional mouse offering many high end features that make it very good value for money. One of these features includes an optical sensor allowing for up to 12,000 dpi if you wish. The sensor is highly accurate and feels very responsive to use, and the Logitech Gaming Software suite allows you to tune the sensor specifically to the surface that you are using to further increase the accuracy of the sensor. Another useful feature that the G502 has is that it is very comfortable to use because of its design, providing a large body for you to grip and very well designed button placements that are easy to access no matter the grip that you prefer to use. And finally the price of the G502 is low for the quality that is offered, retailing at around Â£50 making it available to people on a budget and also people that are looking for a high quality mouse that doesn't break the bank.

TL;DR

  • High quality sensor with up to 12,000 DPI.
  • Ergonomic, easy to access buttons and feels good to use.
  • Cheap, very high build quality for the price.

The Bad

The G502 is a very large mouse, meaning that some people with smaller hands might have issues accessing some of the buttons, such as the sniper button, as they are placed towards the edge of the mouse. Another issue is that the G502 will probably not appeal to the average consumer, it being pushed towards people that are gamers instead of people that are working within an office environment.

TL;DR

  • The sniper button is placed poorly.
  • Doesn't appeal to the average consumer pushed towards gamers.

Conclusion

So, is the G502 worth your money? In my opinion yes it is. The ergonomics are great and it very cheap for the features on offer.
